ClipBack Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 13, 2026

ClipBack is a screen instant-replay app. This policy is short because the honest answer is simple: ClipBack does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any of your data.

What ClipBack records

While you have ClipBack switched on, it keeps a rolling buffer of the most recent seconds of your screen in your phone's memory (RAM) only. The buffer is continuously overwritten. Nothing is written to storage until you explicitly tap Save, at which point a video clip is saved to your own device's gallery (Movies/ClipBack on Android, the Photos library on iPhone). When you stop ClipBack, the buffer is discarded.

What leaves your phone

Nothing. The Android app requests no internet permission, which means the operating system itself prevents it from sending any data anywhere. We have no servers, no analytics, no advertising SDKs, and no crash reporters. Recordings never leave your device unless you personally share a saved clip.

What we collect about you

Nothing. No accounts, no email addresses, no identifiers, no usage statistics.

Purchases

ClipBack Pro subscriptions are processed entirely by Google Play or Apple's App Store. We never see your payment details. Google's and Apple's own privacy policies govern those transactions.

Sensitive content & passwords

Because ClipBack captures whatever is on your screen, treat it like a screen recorder: the operating system shows a visible recording indicator and a persistent notification whenever ClipBack is active, and you can stop it at any time. Android and iOS automatically hide certain protected content (such as DRM-protected video and many password fields) from screen capture.

Children

ClipBack is not directed at children under 13 and collects no data from anyone.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the new version will be posted at this address with an updated date. Because the app has no network access, it cannot change what it does without an app update you approve through the store.
